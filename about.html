Origin story

It started with a CD-R and a Sharpie.

Before Made You A Mixtape was a podcast, it was a habit: burning CDs for friends with long, over-explained liner notes about why a song belonged on the tape. Nobody asked for the essay. Everyone read it anyway.

The show is the same idea, given more room. One song per episode — its history, the choices made in the room it was recorded in, and the reason it still means something decades later. No rankings, no Top 40 lists, no rushing to the next track.

Behind the mic

Hosted by someone who reads liner notes for fun.

Made You A Mixtape is written, researched, and hosted independently — a one-person operation with a very full shelf of records and an even fuller collection of half-finished playlists.

Every episode takes roughly two weeks to research: tracking down interviews, session details, and the odd rights-cleared sound clip that makes the story click into place.

How an episode comes together

From shortlist to final mix.

Pick the song

Usually a listener suggestion, sometimes a personal obsession that's been waiting its turn.

Chase the history

Session notes, old interviews, liner credits, and the occasional cold-call to someone who was in the room.

Pull the sound apart

Isolated stems and close listening, to find the one production choice worth building an episode around.

Write the story

Everything gets scripted — this isn't a ramble, it's a story with a shape.

Record, mix, release

One take, minor edits, out to the feed on a two-week cycle.
